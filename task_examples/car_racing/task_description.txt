Write a Python function that acts as a control policy for an agent in a
top-down car racing environment.

Environment overview:
- The environment is a continuous-control racing task.
- The goal is to cover as much of the track as possible before the step limit.
- The policy controls steering, throttle, and brake.
- The car is a strong rear-wheel-drive vehicle, so aggressive steering together
  with acceleration can easily destabilize it.
- Some visible track fragments may belong to later parts of the course rather
  than the immediately drivable path, so the controller should not react to all
  visible road pixels equally.
- Coming to a full stop is usually harmful because it can prevent the car from
  finishing the course efficiently.

Observation details:
- The observation is a 96x96x3 RGB image.
- The car is red.
- The track is gray.
- Off-track grass is green.
- Curbs in sharp turns are red and white.

Inputs at each step:
- `observation`: the current RGB observation with shape `(96, 96, 3)`.
- `car_speed`: the current scalar speed of the car.
- `pre_action`: the action used at the previous step.
- `pre_observation`: the previous RGB observation.

Output:
- Return a NumPy array of shape `(3,)`:
  - index 0: steering in `[-1, 1]`
  - index 1: gas in `[0, 1]`
  - index 2: brake in `[0, 1]`

Goal:
- Design a control policy that keeps the car on the track and maximizes track
  completion.
